## 1. What FundVella helps with (the product matrix)

FundVella surfaces the funding option that fits how a business actually makes money. Primary is
revenue-based working capital; the rest are matched by profile.

| Product | Who it fits | Public framing facts |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue-Based Funding (MCA)** — *primary* | Active deposits, needs speed, credit-flexible | Purchase of **future receivables**. Cost is a **factor rate → one total payback amount**, not an APR. Daily or weekly remittance. No tax returns. |
| **Line of Credit** | Flexible, recurring needs; cleaner profile | Revolving **$250K–$500K**; draw and repay as needed; pay only for what you draw. |
| **HELOC** | **650+ FICO + real-estate equity** | Line against property equity. **Often funded within a day.** Indicative number up front; final terms confirm against equity & title. Speed is the draw. |
| **Term Loan** | Stronger credit, predictable need, room for monthly | A real loan — **APR from ~4.99%**, **$10K–$10M**, **1–10 yr**, no prepay penalty. APR language is correct here. |
| **SBA Loan** | Top profiles, patient timeline | Bank-funded term loan; **requires tax returns**; slower. |
| **Equipment Financing** | Acquiring machinery/vehicles/tech/furniture | Equipment is collateral → easier to qualify. **Up to 100% financing, up to 7-yr terms.** Possible write-offs (confirm with a CPA). |
| **Bridge Funding** | Needs capital now while a slower loan (SBA/term) is in process | Short-term bridge that can be cashed out when the long-term loan funds. |
| **Invoice Factoring / Asset-Based** | Has receivables or a specific asset | Funding against receivables/assets. |
| **Credit Repair** *(path, not a loan)* | Not fundable yet (e.g. sub-500 FICO / credit-blocked) | White-labeled restoration partner: 3-bureau forensic audit, professional disputes, dedicated manager, client portal. **CROA rails — see §5.** |
| **Card Processing** *(service)* | Existing/operating merchants | Payment processing offering; supportive, never the headline. |

**Funding envelope (advertised):** **$5K–$10M** · approvals in **~1 hour** · **same-day funding possible**.

## 2. The fit philosophy (this is the "truth" the site must express)

- **Match the funding to how the business earns.** Strong, steady **cash flow → Revenue-Based / LOC**.
  **Good credit (650+) + real-estate equity → HELOC** (faster & lighter than Term/SBA). Term/SBA are
  last-resort and slow.
- **Funders pay for *steady, predictable* cash flow — not size.** A steady operator beats a
  higher-but-swingy earner. Steadiness is the asset.
- **Speed and fit over one-size-fits-all.** The right product, matched to the file, beats a generic loan.
- **"Not a no — a not-yet."** If a business doesn't qualify today, there's a path back (credit-repair
  runway, then revisit). Never a dead end.

## 3. Qualification (consumer-facing "what helps you qualify")

The single floor — frame as helpful guidance, never as a hard rejection:

- **$15K+/month** revenue (recent)
- **6+ months** in business
- **500+ FICO** (revenue does the heavy lifting; credit-flexible)
- A **business bank account**

**Documents:** the core is a short application + owner ID + EIN/ownership + **3 months of business
bank statements** + a voided check. (Tax returns only for bank-funded products like SBA.)
*(This is why the site says **3 months** of statements — not 3–4.)*

## 4. Underwriting reality — INTERNAL guidance (shapes journeys/expectations, NOT public "we reject" copy)

These inform routing, expectation-setting, and which angle a page leads with. **Do not** turn them
into public "we don't fund X" statements — every vertical page is still a lead path.

- Revenue-based funders look at: **3+ deposits/month**, **few negative days (~≤4–5/mo)**, **positive
  monthly ending balances** across the last 3 months.
- **Harder to place:** trucking (usually needs an existing position) and Texas (limited appetite).
  → For these, lead with the right qualifier/angle rather than over-promising.
- A current default with **650+ FICO + equity** is a HELOC opportunity (clear the default), not a dead end.

## 5. Compliance rails (MANDATORY on every relevant surface)

- **Revenue-Based Funding / MCA is a purchase of future receivables, not a loan.** Cost = **factor
  rate → total payback**. **Never** "interest rate," "APR," or "bank loan" for it. (APR *is* correct for
  Term Loan / SBA — they are real loans.)
- **Never imply guaranteed approval.** Underwriting decides. Avoid "guaranteed."
- **No specific rate/number before statements.** Offers are quoted after review. Site shows ranges/how
  it works, not a promised number.
- **Always:** no obligation; you see the full payback before you decide.
- **Credit Repair (CROA):** no upfront fees (billed monthly, in arrears), cancel within 3 business days,
  **never promise a score, a number of deletions, or any outcome** ("we dispute inaccurate or
  questionable items").
- **FundVella does not lend / set rates / approve funding** — it connects owners with specialists.
